Light Detection Sensor Circuit

This academic project involved the complete design and simulation of a smart lighting system. The core of the project was to develop a circuit that could accurately detect ambient light levels and automatically adjust artificial lighting to maintain optimal illumination while minimizing energy consumption.

Key Features:

  • Integration of LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) for light sensing.
  • Control logic implemented to switch lighting on/off based on preset thresholds.
  • Simulation performed to validate circuit performance and energy savings.
  • Focus on creating an efficient and cost-effective solution for automated lighting.

Power Systems Analysis

This project utilized MATLAB and Simulink to create a comprehensive model of a power distribution network. The objective was to analyze system behavior under various fault conditions and to understand the flow of power throughout the network.

Key Activities:

  • Modeled various types of faults (e.g., line-to-ground, line-to-line).
  • Performed load flow analysis to determine voltage levels and power distribution.
  • Applied graph theory to simplify and visualize the complex network topology.
  • Generated detailed reports and visualizations of system performance under stress.
